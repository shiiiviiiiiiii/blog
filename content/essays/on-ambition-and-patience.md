---
title: "On Ambition and Patience"
date: "2026-05-14"
excerpt: "I used to think ambition meant moving fast. I'm starting to think it means moving correctly."
---

For a long time, I thought ambition was a speed. The faster you moved, the more ambitious you were. Every year without a visible leap felt like falling behind some invisible schedule I never agreed to.

I don't think that anymore.

I've started noticing that the people I admire most — the ones who built things that lasted — rarely talk about speed. They talk about direction. They talk about the years they spent doing work nobody was watching. The unglamorous middle.

There's a strange comfort in this, once it settles in. It means the years that feel slow aren't wasted. They're load-bearing. You don't see the foundation of a building once it's finished, but it's the reason the building is still standing.

I still want things. I still feel the pull toward more — more skill, more clarity, more impact. But I'm trying to hold that wanting loosely. To let it point me in a direction without demanding I arrive somewhere by a date I made up.

Patience isn't the absence of ambition. It might be its most mature form.

---

Sometimes I wonder if the anxiety around "wasted time" is really just a fear of being ordinary for a while. Of not having a story to tell yet. But every story has a part before the part people remember. Nobody skips to the ending.

I'm trying to be okay with writing the part before the part.
