## Who I am

I'm Shivam Kumar. I'm a student, occasionally an organizer, and on certain evenings, an actor. Most of the time I'm just someone trying to pay closer attention to my own life than I used to.

## What I care about

People, mostly. The way they tell stories, the things they don't say directly, the small decisions that quietly shape who they become. I also care about doing things slowly enough to actually understand them — whether that's a line in a play, a piece of code, or a conversation that could have ended five minutes earlier.

## Why I write

Because I forget things if I don't. Because writing is the only way I've found to actually finish a thought instead of just having it. And because some of what I notice doesn't feel real until it's written down — as if the noticing and the writing are two halves of the same act.

## What success means to me

Less than it used to. I think for a long time I confused success with being impressive. Lately it feels more like: doing work I'd be willing to redo, being someone people feel comfortable being honest with, and having a life that doesn't need a highlight reel to feel worthwhile.

## Things I'm curious about

Why some rooms feel different from others. How people decide what to remember. What theatre and software have in common (more than you'd think). And whatever I happen to be noticing this week — which is usually the real answer to this question.
